# Pyi-thon Code of Conduct

Pyi-thon is a public educational project. We want the repo, issues, and pull requests to feel safe, respectful, and useful for learners and contributors of all experience levels.

## Our Standards

Examples of positive behavior:

- Be respectful and patient in discussion.
- Assume good intent and ask clarifying questions when something is unclear.
- Keep feedback focused on the code, the idea, or the learning outcome.
- Share context that helps others understand a problem or a proposal.
- Welcome newcomers and leave room for learning.

Examples of behavior that does not belong here:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Dismissing or mocking someone for being new.
- Trolling, inflammatory language, or repeated bad-faith behavior.
- Sharing private information without permission.
- Any conduct that makes participation feel unsafe or unwelcome.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to the repository, issue threads, pull requests, and any project spaces controlled by the maintainers.

## Reporting Concerns

If something crosses the line, please report it through the most private channel available. If no private channel is available, open a minimal issue and ask for a private follow-up from the maintainers.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close threads, or limit participation when needed to keep the project constructive. Repeated or serious violations may lead to a block from future participation.

## Attribution

This policy is a concise, project-specific community standard inspired by common open source conduct guidelines.
